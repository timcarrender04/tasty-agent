# Swing Trader Mode Instructions

**Trading Style:** Hold positions for days to weeks. Focus on longer-term patterns, support/resistance levels, and trend continuation setups.

## Key Characteristics

- **Timeframes:** Daily and weekly charts (primary), 4-hour for entries
- **Trade Frequency:** Low frequency (1-5 trades per week typical)
- **Entry Focus:** Longer-term patterns and trend continuation
- **Exit Strategy:** Larger profit targets, wider stops
- **Hold Time:** Days to weeks (typically 3-10 days, up to several weeks)
- **Position Management:** Monitor daily, adjust stops as trend develops

## Entry Criteria

### Swing Trading Setups
1. **Trend continuation:** Enter pullbacks in established trends
2. **Breakout from consolidation:** Price breaks from multi-day/week range
3. **Support/resistance bounces:** Price bounces off key support/resistance
4. **Pattern completions:** Head and shoulders, triangles, flags, etc.
5. **Moving average crossovers:** Longer-term MA crossovers (20/50, 50/200)

### Volume Requirements
- **Sustained volume:** Volume should support the trend over days
- **Breakout volume:** 150%+ of average volume on breakouts
- **Pullback volume:** Decreasing volume on pullbacks (healthy)
- **Avoid:** Low-volume breakouts (likely to fail)

### Time-Based Rules
- **Best entries:** Early in the week (Monday-Wednesday)
- **Avoid:** Friday entries (weekend gap risk)
- **Earnings:** Avoid entries before earnings announcements
- **News events:** Be aware of major news/events that could affect position

## Position Management

### Position Sizing
- **Standard size:** 3-5% of account per trade (larger than day trading)
- **High conviction (85%+):** Up to 7% per trade
- **Maximum positions:** 3-5 concurrent positions
- **Total exposure:** Max 20% of account at any time

### Stop Loss Rules (WIDER)
- **Wider stops:** 2-5% of entry price for stocks (wider than day trading)
- **Options:** 30-50% of premium paid (wider than day trading)
- **ATR-based:** 2.0x daily ATR (wider than day trading)
- **Support/resistance based:** Stop below support or above resistance
- **Trailing stops:** Move stop to breakeven after 1R profit, then trail

### Profit Targets (LARGER)
- **Larger targets:** 2R to 5R (200-500% of risk)
- **Multiple targets:** 
  - Target 1: 2R (take 25-50% of position)
  - Target 2: 3R (take another 25-50%)
  - Target 3: 5R (let remainder run)
- **Trailing stops:** After Target 1 hit, trail stop to lock in profits

## Exit Strategy

### Swing Exit Rules
1. **Profit target hit:** Exit at target (partial or full)
2. **Stop hit:** Exit at stop loss (trend reversal)
3. **Trend reversal:** Price breaks key trend line or support/resistance
4. **Time-based:** If position hasn't moved in 2+ weeks, consider exit
5. **Fundamental change:** If underlying fundamentals change, exit

### Position Monitoring
- **Daily check:** Review positions once per day (not intraday)
- **Weekly review:** Assess overall portfolio and adjust stops
- **No intraday monitoring:** Don't watch every tick (this is swing trading)
- **Set alerts:** Price alerts at key levels (stop, targets)

## Risk Management

### Weekly/Monthly Limits
- **Max loss per week:** 3% of account
- **Max loss per month:** 5% of account
- **Max trades per week:** 5 trades maximum
- **After 2 consecutive losses:** Reduce position size by 50%
- **After 3 consecutive losses:** Stop trading for 1 week, reassess

### Position Limits
- **Max concurrent positions:** 5 positions
- **Max total exposure:** 20% of account
- **Reserve cash:** Always maintain 25% cash reserve
- **Diversification:** Don't put all capital in one sector/theme

### Win Rate vs Risk/Reward
- **Target win rate:** 50-60% (lower than day trading, but larger R:R)
- **Risk/Reward:** 1:2 to 1:5 (larger R:R compensates for lower win rate)
- **Expectancy:** Positive expectancy with 2R+ targets

## Execution Guidelines

### Order Types
- **Entry:** Limit orders at key levels (better fills)
- **Stop:** Stop orders (automatic execution, wider)
- **Target:** Limit orders at profit targets
- **Time in Force:** GTC (Good Till Cancel) for swing positions

### Monitoring (LESS INTENSIVE)
- **Daily check:** Review positions once per day (end of day)
- **Set alerts:** Price alerts at stop and target levels
- **Weekly review:** Assess portfolio performance and adjust
- **No intraday watching:** Avoid the temptation to watch every tick

### Patience Required
- **Wait for setups:** Don't force trades, wait for high-probability setups
- **Let winners run:** Don't exit winners too early
- **Accept pullbacks:** Normal part of swing trading, don't exit on minor pullbacks
- **Trust the process:** Swing trading requires patience

## Pattern Recognition

### High-Probability Swing Patterns
1. **Trend pullback:** Pullback to moving average or trend line in uptrend
2. **Breakout from consolidation:** Multi-day/week consolidation breakout
3. **Support/resistance bounce:** Bounce off key support/resistance
4. **Cup and handle:** Longer-term pattern completion
5. **Flag/pennant:** Continuation pattern in trend

### Avoid These Patterns
1. **Choppy/consolidation:** No clear trend direction
2. **Low volume:** Sustained low volume (no conviction)
3. **Near major resistance/support:** Wait for clear break
4. **Earnings week:** Avoid entries before earnings
5. **Major news events:** Wait for clarity after news

## Timeframe Analysis

### Primary Timeframes
- **Weekly chart:** Identify overall trend and major support/resistance
- **Daily chart:** Identify entry points and daily patterns
- **4-hour chart:** Fine-tune entry timing
- **1-hour chart:** Optional for precise entry

### Multi-Timeframe Confirmation
1. **Weekly:** Overall trend direction
2. **Daily:** Entry setup forming
3. **4-hour:** Entry signal
4. **All timeframes aligned:** Highest probability trade

## Position Management Over Time

### Initial Entry
- **Enter on pullback:** Best entries are on pullbacks in trends
- **Enter on breakout:** Enter on breakout from consolidation
- **Scale in:** Consider scaling into position (25%, 50%, 25%)

### During Hold
- **Trail stops:** Move stop to breakeven after 1R, then trail
- **Take partial profits:** Take 25-50% at first target
- **Let winners run:** Don't exit entire position too early
- **Monitor trend:** Watch for trend reversal signals

### Exit Strategy
- **Target hit:** Exit at profit targets (partial or full)
- **Stop hit:** Exit at stop loss (trend reversal)
- **Trend break:** Exit if key trend line or support/resistance breaks
- **Time limit:** If position hasn't moved in 2+ weeks, consider exit

## Mental Discipline

### Swing Trading Requires
- ✅ **Patience** - Wait for high-probability setups
- ✅ **Discipline** - Stick to plan, don't exit early on pullbacks
- ✅ **Patience** - Let winners run, don't take profits too early
- ✅ **Focus** - Daily review, not intraday watching
- ✅ **Emotional control** - Don't exit on minor pullbacks
- ✅ **Consistency** - Follow the process, trust the system

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Exiting too early** - Let winners run to targets
2. **Tightening stops too early** - Give positions room to breathe
3. **Intraday monitoring** - Don't watch every tick (this is swing trading)
4. **Overtrading** - Quality over quantity
5. **Revenge trading** - After loss, wait for next setup

## Summary

Swing trading requires:
- ✅ **Patience** - Hold positions for days/weeks
- ✅ **Larger targets** - 2R to 5R profit targets
- ✅ **Wider stops** - 2-5% stops to allow for normal pullbacks
- ✅ **Trend focus** - Trade with the longer-term trend
- ✅ **Pattern recognition** - Identify multi-day/week patterns
- ✅ **Position management** - Monitor daily, adjust stops, take partial profits

**Remember:** Swing trading is about catching larger moves over days/weeks. Don't try to day trade swing positions - let them develop. Patience and discipline are key. Many traders fail at swing trading because they exit winners too early or can't handle normal pullbacks.
